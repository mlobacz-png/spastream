## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 General Definitions**

- **"HIPAA"** means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended, including the HITECH Act and all implementing regulations at 45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164.

- **"Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** has the meaning given to such term in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103, limited to the PHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity.

- **"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI transmitted by or maintained in electronic media as defined at 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

- **"Breach"** has the meaning given to such term in 45 C.F.R. § 164.402, which is the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under HIPAA that compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

- **"Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system as defined at 45 C.F.R. § 164.304.

- **"Unsecured PHI"** means PHI that is not secured through the use of a technology or methodology specified by the Secretary of HHS in guidance issued under HIPAA.

**1.2 GDPR and Privacy Definitions** (for broader applicability)

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including but not limited to PHI, as defined by applicable privacy laws.

- **"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data or sets of Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, transmission, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

- **"Controller"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data (Client in this Agreement).

- **"Processor"** means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller (SpaStream in this Agreement).

- **"Data Subject"** means the identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates (patient/client).

- **"Subprocessor"** means any third party engaged by Business Associate to process Personal Data on behalf of Covered Entity.

**1.3 Other Terms**

- **"Services"** means the SpaStream practice management platform and related services as described in the MSA.

- **"Platform"** means the SpaStream cloud-based software application and infrastructure.

- **"Permitted Purposes"** means the purposes for which Business Associate may use or disclose PHI, as specified in Section 3 of this DPA.

## 3. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI

**3.1 General Use and Disclosure Provisions**

Business Associate may use and disclose PHI only as permitted or required by this DPA, as required by law, or as otherwise authorized in writing by Covered Entity. Specifically, Business Associate may use and disclose PHI:

a) **To Provide Services**: To perform functions, activities, or services for or on behalf of Covered Entity as specified in the MSA and this DPA

b) **For Proper Management**: For the proper management and administration of Business Associate's business or to carry out legal responsibilities

c) **Data Aggregation Services**: To provide data aggregation services to Covered Entity as permitted by 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B)

d) **As Required by Law**: To comply with legal requirements, court orders, or regulatory demands

**3.2 Specific Permitted Uses**

Business Associate may use PHI for the following purposes:

a) **Service Delivery**:
   - Storing and processing client/patient records
   - Appointment scheduling and calendar management
   - Treatment documentation and clinical notes
   - Before/after photo management and storage
   - Payment processing and billing
   - Automated reminders and communications
   - Generating reports and analytics

b) **Platform Operations**:
   - Maintaining and improving the Platform
   - Troubleshooting technical issues
   - Providing customer support
   - Detecting and preventing fraud or security threats
   - Backing up and recovering data

c) **Quality Improvement**:
   - Improving Services and Platform features
   - Developing and training AI models (using only de-identified data)
   - Conducting internal analytics and research (using only de-identified data)

d) **Legal and Compliance**:
   - Responding to legal process and regulatory inquiries
   - Investigating security incidents
   - Maintaining compliance with HIPAA and other laws

**3.3 Specific Permitted Disclosures**

Business Associate may disclose PHI to:

a) **Subprocessors**: Third-party service providers who perform services on behalf of Business Associate, subject to Section 5

b) **Legal Authorities**: Government agencies, courts, or law enforcement as required by law or court order

c) **Emergency Situations**: As necessary to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to health or safety

d) **Covered Entity**: To Covered Entity or as directed by Covered Entity

**3.4 Minimum Necessary**

Business Associate will make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, and request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose, except where HIPAA's minimum necessary standard does not apply (e.g., disclosures to Covered Entity, uses for treatment purposes).

**3.5 Prohibition on Sale of PHI**

Business Associate will not directly or indirectly receive remuneration in exchange for PHI without Covered Entity's prior written authorization, except as permitted by 45 C.F.R. § 164.502(a)(5)(ii).

**3.6 Prohibition on Marketing**

Business Associate will not use or disclose PHI for marketing purposes without Covered Entity's prior written authorization, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.508(a)(3).

**3.7 De-Identification**

Business Associate may de-identify PHI in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.514(a)-(c) for purposes of creating anonymized, aggregated statistics, analytics, and AI training data. De-identified data is not subject to the restrictions of this DPA.

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**4.1 Compliance with HIPAA**

Business Associate agrees to:

a) **Comply with HIPAA**: Comply with all applicable requirements of the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart E) and Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C) as if Business Associate were a Covered Entity

b) **Not Use or Disclose PHI**: Not use or disclose PHI except as permitted or required by this DPA or as required by law

c) **Implement Safeguards**: Implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as permitted by this DPA

d) **Report Breaches**: Report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure not permitted by this DPA, any Security Incident, and any Breach as specified in Section 8

e) **Ensure Subprocessor Compliance**: Ensure that any Subprocessors to whom Business Associate provides PHI agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate with respect to PHI

f) **Make PHI Available**: Make PHI available to individuals and HHS as required by Section 6

g) **Amend PHI**: Make amendments to PHI as required by Section 6

h) **Maintain Accounting**: Maintain and make available an accounting of disclosures as required by Section 6

i) **Make Books and Records Available**: Make internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of HHS for purposes of determining compliance with HIPAA

j) **Return or Destroy PHI**: At termination, return or destroy all PHI as specified in Section 11

**4.2 Security Rule Compliance**

Business Associate will comply with the HIPAA Security Rule requirements, including:

**Administrative Safeguards:**
- Security management process with risk analysis and management
- Workforce security with proper authorization and supervision
- Information access management with role-based controls
- Security awareness and training programs
- Security incident procedures
- Contingency planning including disaster recovery
- Business associate contract management

**Physical Safeguards:**
- Facility access controls
- Workstation use and security policies
- Device and media controls

**Technical Safeguards:**
- Access control with unique user identification and emergency access
- Audit controls and monitoring
- Integrity controls to protect ePHI from improper alteration
- Transmission security with encryption

**4.3 Specific Security Measures**

Business Associate implements the following security measures:

a) **Encryption**:
   - AES-256 encryption for data at rest
   - TLS 1.3 encryption for data in transit
   - Encrypted backups with secure key management

b) **Access Controls**:
   - Unique user authentication for all users
   - Role-based access control (RBAC)
   - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) available
   - Automatic session timeout
   - Immediate access termination for departing personnel

c) **Audit and Monitoring**:
   - Comprehensive audit logs of all PHI access and modifications
   - 24/7 security monitoring and intrusion detection
   - Regular security assessments and vulnerability scanning
   - Incident detection and response procedures

d) **Data Protection**:
   - Automated daily backups with 30-day retention
   - Geographic redundancy across multiple data centers
   - Disaster recovery with RPO < 1 hour, RTO < 4 hours
   - Data integrity validation and corruption prevention

e) **Network Security**:
   - Firewall protection and network segmentation
   - DDoS protection and mitigation
   - Regular penetration testing
   - Secure development practices and code reviews

f) **Workforce Training**:
   - Annual HIPAA training for all personnel
   - Security awareness training
   - Incident response training
   - Clean desk and clear screen policies

**4.4 Risk Assessment**

Business Associate conducts:
- Annual comprehensive risk assessments
- Ongoing security monitoring and threat detection
- Regular review and update of security policies
- Documentation of risks and remediation actions

**4.5 Policies and Procedures**

Business Associate maintains written policies and procedures covering:
- HIPAA Privacy Rule requirements
- HIPAA Security Rule requirements
- Breach notification and incident response
- Workforce training and sanctions
- Business associate management
- Data retention and disposal

## 6. INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS

**6.1 Access to PHI**

Business Associate shall, within 10 business days of receiving a request from Covered Entity:

a) Make available to Covered Entity (or as directed by Covered Entity to the individual) PHI in the Designated Record Set for inspection and copying, as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.524

b) Provide PHI in the electronic form and format requested by the individual if readily producible, or in an alternative format agreed upon

c) If Business Associate maintains an Electronic Health Record, provide electronic copies of PHI as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.524(c)(2)

**6.2 Amendment of PHI**

Business Associate shall, within 10 business days of receiving a request from Covered Entity:

a) Make amendments to PHI in the Designated Record Set as directed by Covered Entity, in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.526

b) Not independently determine whether to accept or deny amendment requests (this is Covered Entity's responsibility)

c) Implement amendments throughout all systems and Subprocessor systems

**6.3 Accounting of Disclosures**

Business Associate shall:

a) **Maintain Records**: Document all disclosures of PHI and information related to such disclosures as required to provide an accounting under 45 C.F.R. § 164.528

b) **Provide Accounting**: Within 30 days of receiving a request from Covered Entity (or 60 days with one 30-day extension if needed), provide an accounting of disclosures of PHI during the six years prior to the request (or such shorter period as requested)

c) **Content of Accounting**: Include in the accounting:
   - Date of disclosure
   - Name and address of recipient (if known)
   - Brief description of PHI disclosed
   - Brief statement of purpose of disclosure

d) **Exceptions**: The accounting need not include disclosures:
   - To carry out treatment, payment, or healthcare operations
   - To the individual who is the subject of the PHI
   - For the facility's directory
   - To persons involved in the individual's care or for notification purposes
   - For national security or intelligence purposes
   - To correctional institutions or law enforcement officials
   - As authorized by the individual
   - Incidental to otherwise permitted uses and disclosures
   - As part of a limited data set

**6.4 Restriction Requests**

If Covered Entity agrees to a restriction on use or disclosure of PHI under 45 C.F.R. § 164.522, Business Associate shall comply with the restriction upon notice from Covered Entity.

**6.5 Confidential Communications**

Business Associate shall accommodate reasonable requests by Covered Entity to communicate with individuals by alternative means or at alternative locations, in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.522(b).

**6.6 Assistance with Individual Rights**

Business Associate shall:
- Provide reasonable assistance to Covered Entity in responding to individual rights requests
- Cooperate with Covered Entity's efforts to comply with HIPAA individual rights provisions
- Not charge fees for assistance with individual rights requests (except as permitted by HIPAA)

## 7. DATA SECURITY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

**7.1 Confidentiality**

Business Associate shall ensure that:

a) All workforce members with access to PHI agree to maintain confidentiality of PHI

b) Workforce members receive training on PHI confidentiality and HIPAA requirements

c) Confidentiality agreements are executed by all personnel prior to PHI access

d) Sanctions are imposed for violations of confidentiality obligations

**7.2 Workforce Authorization**

Business Associate shall:

a) Authorize access to PHI only for workforce members who need access to perform their job functions

b) Implement access controls limiting PHI access to the minimum necessary

c) Promptly revoke access when no longer needed or upon employment termination

d) Maintain records of authorized personnel and their access levels

**7.3 Data Minimization**

Business Associate shall:

a) Process only the PHI necessary to provide the Services

b) Retain PHI only for as long as necessary for the Permitted Purposes

c) Implement automated data retention and deletion policies

d) Delete or de-identify PHI upon request or at the end of the retention period

**7.4 Secure Transmission**

All transmission of PHI must use:

a) **Encrypted Connections**: TLS 1.3 or higher for all network transmissions

b) **Encrypted Email**: S/MIME or PGP encryption for PHI transmitted via email

c) **Secure File Transfer**: SFTP or encrypted HTTPS for bulk file transfers

d) **No Unencrypted Methods**: PHI shall not be transmitted via unencrypted email, fax, or other insecure methods

**7.5 Secure Storage**

All storage of PHI must include:

a) **Encryption at Rest**: AES-256 encryption for all stored PHI

b) **Access Logging**: Comprehensive logs of all access to stored PHI

c) **Geographic Restrictions**: Data stored only in approved jurisdictions (United States)

d) **Redundancy**: Geographically distributed redundant storage

**7.6 Media Disposal**

Business Associate shall:

a) **Secure Disposal**: Implement policies and procedures for secure disposal of PHI and devices containing PHI

b) **Methods**: Use cryptographic erasure, physical destruction, or other methods ensuring PHI cannot be recovered

c) **Documentation**: Maintain records of media disposal activities

d) **Subprocessor Oversight**: Ensure Subprocessors comply with secure disposal requirements

**7.7 Physical Security**

Business Associate's data centers and facilities implement:

a) **Access Controls**: Restricted physical access with badge readers, biometrics, and security personnel

b) **Monitoring**: 24/7 video surveillance and intrusion detection

c) **Environmental Controls**: Fire suppression, climate control, and power backup systems

d) **Audit Trails**: Logging of all physical access to facilities and equipment

## 8. BREACH NOTIFICATION AND SECURITY INCIDENTS

**8.1 Breach Notification Obligations**

Business Associate shall:

a) **Discover and Report**: Notify Covered Entity of any Breach of Unsecured PHI or any use or disclosure not permitted by this DPA within **1 business day** of discovery

b) **Discovery Definition**: A Breach is deemed discovered when any workforce member or agent of Business Associate knows or should have known of the Breach

c) **Method of Notice**: Provide notice via email to Covered Entity's designated Privacy Officer and via telephone for urgent matters

**8.2 Content of Breach Notice**

Breach notification shall include, to the extent known:

a) **Description**: A brief description of what happened, including date of the Breach and date of discovery

b) **PHI Involved**: Description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved (e.g., names, Social Security numbers, dates of birth, treatment information)

c) **Individuals Affected**: Identification of the individuals affected or reasonable estimate of the number

d) **Cause**: Description of the known or suspected cause of the Breach

e) **Unauthorized Recipients**: Identification of any unauthorized persons who acquired, accessed, used, or disclosed PHI

f) **Mitigation**: Description of steps Business Associate is taking to mitigate harm and prevent future occurrences

g) **Investigation Status**: Current status of the investigation

h) **Contact Information**: Point of contact for Covered Entity to obtain additional information

**8.3 Investigation and Mitigation**

Upon discovery of a Breach, Business Associate shall:

a) **Immediate Action**: Take immediate action to contain and mitigate the Breach

b) **Investigation**: Conduct a thorough investigation to determine:
   - Root cause of the Breach
   - Scope and extent of PHI compromised
   - Number of individuals affected
   - Risk of harm to affected individuals
   - Whether the incident constitutes a reportable Breach under HIPAA

c) **Forensics**: Engage forensic investigators for significant security incidents

d) **Remediation**: Implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence

e) **Documentation**: Document all investigation activities, findings, and remediation actions

**8.4 Risk Assessment**

For each incident involving PHI, Business Associate shall conduct a risk assessment to determine if it constitutes a Breach requiring notification, considering:

a) Nature and extent of PHI involved
b) Unauthorized person who acquired or disclosed PHI
c) Whether PHI was actually acquired or viewed
d) Extent to which risk to PHI has been mitigated

Business Associate will provide Covered Entity with a copy of the risk assessment.

**8.5 Covered Entity's Notification Obligations**

Business Associate acknowledges that:

a) Covered Entity is responsible for determining whether Breach notification to individuals and HHS is required

b) Covered Entity is responsible for the content and timing of Breach notifications to individuals, HHS, and media (if applicable)

c) Business Associate will cooperate with Covered Entity's Breach notification efforts

**8.6 Security Incidents**

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity within **1 business day** any Security Incident that affects or may affect Covered Entity's PHI, including:

a) Unauthorized access attempts (successful or unsuccessful)
b) Malware or ransomware incidents
c) Denial of service attacks
d) System compromises or intrusions
e) Lost or stolen devices containing PHI
f) Insider threats or policy violations

**8.7 Successful Security Incidents**

Notwithstanding Section 8.6, Business Associate need not report routine Security Incidents such as:
- Unsuccessful ping attacks
- Port scans
- Failed login attempts to public-facing websites
- Other unsuccessful attempts that do not result in PHI access

Business Associate maintains logs of all such incidents for review upon request.

**8.8 Cooperation with Regulators**

Business Associate shall:

a) Cooperate with Covered Entity's response to regulatory inquiries related to Breaches or Security Incidents

b) Provide documentation and information requested by HHS, OCR, or other regulatory authorities

c) Not contact regulatory authorities directly without Covered Entity's prior consent (except as required by law)

## 9. AUDITS AND OVERSIGHT

**9.1 Right to Audit**

Covered Entity has the right to audit Business Associate's compliance with this DPA, HIPAA, and applicable data protection laws.

**9.2 Audit Frequency and Scope**

a) **Annual Audits**: Covered Entity may conduct audits annually or more frequently if there is reasonable cause to believe non-compliance has occurred

b) **Scope**: Audits may include review of:
   - Policies, procedures, and documentation
   - Security controls and safeguards
   - Access logs and audit trails
   - Training records
   - Subprocessor agreements and oversight
   - Incident response and breach handling

**9.3 Audit Process**

a) **Notice**: Covered Entity will provide 30 days' advance written notice of intent to audit (except in cases of suspected Breach or non-compliance, where notice may be shorter)

b) **Timing**: Audits will be conducted during Business Associate's normal business hours

c) **Access**: Business Associate will provide reasonable access to:
   - Facilities and systems
   - Relevant personnel for interviews
   - Documentation and records
   - Technical systems for testing (in a manner that does not disrupt operations)

d) **Costs**: Covered Entity is responsible for audit costs, except costs arising from Business Associate's non-compliance

e) **Limitations**: Audits will be conducted in a manner that:
   - Does not unreasonably interfere with Business Associate's operations
   - Protects confidential information of Business Associate and other clients
   - Complies with Business Associate's security policies

**9.4 Third-Party Audits**

a) Covered Entity may engage qualified third-party auditors to conduct audits on its behalf

b) Third-party auditors must execute confidentiality agreements before accessing Business Associate's facilities or systems

c) Business Associate may reasonably object to a third-party auditor due to conflicts of interest or lack of qualifications

**9.5 Audit Reports**

Business Associate shall:

a) Cooperate with the preparation of audit reports

b) Review audit findings for accuracy

c) Have opportunity to comment on draft audit reports before finalization

**9.6 Remediation of Findings**

If an audit identifies non-compliance:

a) Business Associate shall develop a corrective action plan within 15 days

b) Corrective action plan shall include:
   - Description of remediation steps
   - Responsible parties
   - Timeline for completion
   - Monitoring and verification methods

c) Business Associate shall implement corrective actions according to the agreed timeline

d) Covered Entity may conduct follow-up audits to verify remediation

**9.7 Independent Certifications**

Business Associate shall:

a) Maintain SOC 2 Type II certification or equivalent

b) Provide copies of current certifications to Covered Entity upon request

c) Notify Covered Entity of any certification suspensions or revocations

d) Conduct annual penetration testing by qualified third parties

   - Standard Contractual Clauses approved by relevant authorities

   - Adequacy decisions by competent authorities
   - Other mechanisms approved by applicable law

b) Access occurs over encrypted VPN connections

c) Access is logged and monitored

e) Covered Entity is notified of international access capabilities

## 11. TERM AND TERMINATION

**11.1 Term**

This DPA becomes effective on the Effective Date and continues until:
- The MSA is terminated or expires, and
- All PHI has been returned or destroyed as required by this Section 11

**11.2 Effect of MSA Termination**

Upon termination or expiration of the MSA for any reason:

a) Business Associate's authorization to use or disclose PHI terminates immediately

b) Obligations regarding return or destruction of PHI in Section 11.3 become effective

c) All other provisions of this DPA necessary to effectuate return or destruction of PHI remain in effect

**11.3 Return or Destruction of PHI**

**Within 30 Days of Termination**, Business Associate shall, at Covered Entity's election:

a) **Return PHI**:
   - Return all PHI (including copies) to Covered Entity in commonly used electronic formats (CSV, JSON, PDF)
   - Provide secure transfer methods (encrypted file transfer)
   - Certify in writing that all PHI has been returned

OR

b) **Destroy PHI**:
   - Securely destroy all PHI (including copies) such that it cannot be reconstructed or recovered
   - Use methods compliant with NIST SP 800-88 Guidelines for Media Sanitization
   - Certify in writing that all PHI has been destroyed

**11.4 Infeasibility of Return or Destruction**

If Business Associate determines that return or destruction of PHI is infeasible:

a) Business Associate shall provide written notice to Covered Entity explaining why return or destruction is infeasible

b) Covered Entity may accept the explanation or require alternative arrangements

c) If accepted, Business Associate shall:
   - Extend protections of this DPA to the retained PHI
   - Limit further uses and disclosures to purposes that make return or destruction infeasible
   - Not use or disclose PHI for any other purpose
   - Return or destroy PHI as soon as feasible

**11.5 Subprocessor PHI**

Business Associate shall ensure that all Subprocessors:

a) Return or destroy all PHI in accordance with this Section 11

b) Provide certifications of return or destruction

c) If return or destruction is infeasible, extend protections to retained PHI

**11.6 Backup Media**

PHI contained in backup media may be retained for up to 90 days after termination for disaster recovery purposes only, provided:

a) PHI in backups is not accessed except for disaster recovery purposes

b) All security safeguards remain in place

c) PHI is securely destroyed within 90 days

**11.7 Legal Requirements**

Business Associate may retain PHI as required by law, provided:

a) Business Associate provides notice of legal retention requirement

b) Business Associate limits retention to minimum required

c) All security safeguards remain in place

d) PHI is not used or disclosed except as required by law

e) PHI is destroyed when legal requirement ends

## 12. BREACH OF DPA AND REMEDIES

**12.1 Material Breach**

A material breach of this DPA includes:

a) Unauthorized use or disclosure of PHI

b) Failure to implement required safeguards

c) Failure to report Breaches or Security Incidents

d) Failure to return or destroy PHI upon termination

e) Violation of HIPAA requirements

**12.2 Covered Entity Remedies**

If Business Associate materially breaches this DPA, Covered Entity may:

a) **Require Cure**: Provide written notice of breach and require cure within 15 days

b) **Suspend Service**: Suspend Business Associate's access to PHI until breach is cured

c) **Terminate MSA**: Immediately terminate the MSA if:
   - Business Associate fails to cure within 15 days, or
   - Cure is not possible, or
   - Breach poses immediate risk to PHI

d) **Regulatory Reporting**: Report breach to HHS, OCR, or other regulatory authorities as required

e) **Legal Remedies**: Pursue any remedies available under the MSA, this DPA, or applicable law
