# Decision Makers & Outreach Strategy Guide

## Typical Decision Makers in MedSpa/Aesthetic Practices

### 1. **Owner/Medical Director** (Primary Decision Maker - 80% of cases)
**Profile:**
- Usually a physician (MD/DO), nurse practitioner, or physician assistant
- Wears multiple hats: clinical provider, business owner, marketer
- Age range: 35-60
- Concerned with: Revenue, compliance, growth, time savings

**Pain Points:**
- Overwhelmed with administrative work
- Struggling to scale beyond their personal capacity
- Worried about compliance and liability
- Want to increase revenue without working more hours
- Frustrated with staff turnover and training

**Decision Criteria:**
- ROI and revenue impact (most important)
- HIPAA compliance and security
- Ease of implementation (they're busy)
- Quality of support
- Ability to work remotely/on mobile

**Best Messaging:**
- "Get back to patient care, not paperwork"
- "Scale your practice without scaling your stress"
- "Increase revenue by 30-40% without adding hours"

---

### 2. **Practice Manager/Operations Manager** (Influencer - 60% involved)
**Profile:**
- Non-clinical but runs day-to-day operations
- Usually reports to owner
- Age range: 30-50
- Concerned with: Efficiency, staff productivity, client satisfaction

**Pain Points:**
- Managing staff schedules and tasks
- Handling client complaints and no-shows
- Tracking inventory and supplies
- Reporting to ownership on performance
- Training new staff repeatedly

**Decision Criteria:**
- User-friendliness for staff
- Reporting and analytics capabilities
- Integration with existing tools
- Training and onboarding support
- Mobile accessibility for staff

**Best Messaging:**
- "Automate the chaos of daily operations"
- "Empower your team with professional tools"
- "See everything happening in your practice at a glance"

**Important Note:** Often the Practice Manager researches options and recommends to the owner. They may be your initial contact but need ammunition to sell internally.

---

### 3. **Front Desk Manager/Lead Receptionist** (User/Minor Influencer - 40% consulted)
**Profile:**
- First line of client contact
- Handles scheduling, reminders, payments
- Age range: 25-45
- Concerned with: Making their job easier, keeping clients happy

**Pain Points:**
- Manually sending appointment reminders
- Clients not showing up (reflects poorly on them)
- Juggling phone calls while checking people in
- Dealing with payment issues
- Managing cancellations and reschedules

**Decision Criteria:**
- Ease of use (they'll use it most)
- Automated reminders and confirmations
- Quick client lookup and scheduling
- Payment processing simplicity

**Best Messaging:**
- "Spend less time on the phone, more time with clients"
- "Never manually send another reminder"
- "Reduce no-shows by 40%"

**Important Note:** They rarely make final decisions but can kill a deal if they say "this is too hard to use." Get them on your side early.

---

### 4. **Medical Spa Group Owner/Multi-Location Operator** (Different Beast)
**Profile:**
- Owns 2+ locations
- More business-focused, less clinically involved
- Age range: 40-65
- Concerned with: Standardization, scalability, consolidated reporting

**Pain Points:**
- Inconsistent operations across locations
- Can't see real-time performance of each location
- Difficulty scaling processes
- High staff turnover requires constant retraining
- Franchising or selling requires documented systems

**Decision Criteria:**
- Multi-location support
- Consolidated dashboards and reporting
- Role-based permissions
- Brand consistency across locations
- Enterprise-level support

**Best Messaging:**
- "Standardize operations across all locations"
- "See all your locations in one dashboard"
- "Scale your proven playbook effortlessly"

---

### 5. **Purchasing Committee/Group Decision** (Less Common - 15% of cases)
**Profile:**
- Found in larger practices or medical groups
- Includes owner, manager, and sometimes head injector/esthetician
- Decision by consensus

**Pain Points:**
- Different stakeholders care about different things
- Getting everyone to agree is slow
- Previous software failed because not everyone bought in

**Decision Criteria:**
- Must satisfy multiple stakeholders
- Needs buy-in from clinical and administrative staff
- Often requires longer sales cycle
- May need multiple demos for different people

**Best Messaging:**
- Customize messaging for each stakeholder
- Offer group demos
- Provide role-specific value propositions
- Show how it benefits everyone

## Decision-Making Process Timeline

### Small Practice (1-2 providers):
- **Research Phase:** 1-2 weeks
- **Demo/Trial Phase:** 1-2 weeks
- **Decision:** Often made by owner alone
- **Total Sales Cycle:** 2-4 weeks

### Medium Practice (3-5 providers):
- **Research Phase:** 2-4 weeks
- **Demo/Trial Phase:** 2-3 weeks
- **Internal Consensus:** 1-2 weeks
- **Total Sales Cycle:** 5-9 weeks

### Large Practice/Multi-Location (6+ providers):
- **Research Phase:** 4-8 weeks
- **Demo/Trial Phase:** 2-4 weeks
- **Committee Review:** 2-4 weeks
- **Procurement/Contracts:** 1-2 weeks
- **Total Sales Cycle:** 9-18 weeks

> [Your Name]

**Email 2 (3 days later): The Social Proof Email**
Subject: Re: [First Name], losing clients to poor follow-up?

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Just wanted to follow up in case my last email got buried.
>
> I wanted to share a quick win: [Similar Practice Name] in [Location] implemented our platform 6 months ago and they've:
> - Reduced no-shows by 42%
> - Increased rebooking rate from 45% to 68%

>
> Would love to show you how this could work for [Practice Name]. Are you free for a quick call this week?
>
> [Your Name]

**Email 3 (4 days later): The Breakup Email**
Subject: Should I close your file?

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I haven't heard back so I'm assuming this isn't a priority right now (or I've been going to spam - it happens!).
>
> Before I close your file, I wanted to ask:
>

> 2. Or is this just not a fit for [Practice Name]?

> [Your Name]

**Pro Tips:**
- Send Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am local time
- Keep emails under 150 words
- Personalize the first line (mention something specific about their practice)
- One CTA per email
- Use plain text (no HTML) - looks more personal

## Multi-Channel Outreach Sequence (Most Effective)

Don't rely on just one channel. Here's a proven sequence:

### Week 1:
- **Day 1:** LinkedIn connection request
- **Day 3:** LinkedIn message after connection
- **Day 5:** Email #1

### Week 2:
- **Day 8:** Email #2
- **Day 10:** Instagram/Facebook engagement (like/comment on posts)
- **Day 12:** LinkedIn follow-up message

### Week 3:
- **Day 15:** Instagram DM
- **Day 17:** Email #3 (breakup email)
- **Day 19:** Phone call

### Week 4-8:
- Stay top-of-mind with valuable content
- Comment on their social posts
- Share relevant industry insights
- Wait for them to reach back out

**Why This Works:**
- Multiple touchpoints increase familiarity
- Shows you're persistent but not desperate
- Meets them where they're most active
- Provides value throughout

## Red Flags (Time Wasters)

**Don't Waste Time On:**
- "Just browsing" with no pain points
- Practices under 6 months old (too early)
- Owner who just bought NEW software recently (wait 6-12 months)
- "Send me information" with no follow-up meeting (they're not serious)
- Wants custom development before committing
- Won't give you a budget range ("how much does it cost?")

---

## Qualification Questions (Ask Early)

Before you invest time in demos and proposals:

1. **"What problem are you trying to solve?"** (No problem = no urgency)
2. **"What are you using now, and what's not working?"** (Understand their pain)
3. **"If this works, when would you want to be up and running?"** (Timeline/urgency)
4. **"Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"** (Decision-making process)
5. **"What's your budget range for a solution like this?"** (Affordability)

If they can't answer these clearly, they're not ready to buy.

## Final Pro Tips

1. **Personalization wins** - Generic outreach gets ignored. Mention their practice name, specific treatments, or recent Instagram post.

2. **Lead with value, not features** - They don't care about "cloud-based scheduling." They care about "getting 10 hours back per week."

3. **Speak their language** - Use industry terms (injections, facials, laser treatments) to show you understand their world.

4. **Follow up relentlessly** - Most deals happen after 5-7 touchpoints. The fortune is in the follow-up.

5. **Ask for referrals early** - "Even if this isn't a fit for you, who else do you know that might benefit?"

6. **Build relationships, not just sales** - Join their Facebook groups, comment on LinkedIn posts, be a resource. When they're ready to buy, they'll think of you.

7. **Test and track** - Use a CRM to track which channels and messages convert best. Double down on what works.
